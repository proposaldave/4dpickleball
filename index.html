Level 3 — Reset Drop

When you're in trouble — pushed back, off-balance, under pressure — the reset drop buys time. Not a winner. Not a dink. A nothing ball that lands soft in the kitchen and lets you get back to the line.

🔓 Unlock Criteria

○
Complete Level 2 (Baby Block)
○
Attend 2 Reset Drop clinics
○
Demo: 5 consecutive resets from mid-court that land in kitchen
○
Coach confirms soft hands under pressure

Concept The Nothing Ball

Why doing less is more. The reset isn't a shot — it's a pause button.

Drill Mid-Court Resets

Partner feeds drives; you drop into the kitchen from transition zone.

Drill Pressure → Reset → Recover

Full sequence: absorb attack, reset, regain position at the line.

Assessment Reset Under Fire

Partner escalates speed. Maintain soft resets for 8 consecutive balls.

Level 2 — Baby Block

Attacks are coming. The instinct is to swing big. The skill is to do almost nothing — absorb the pace, stay compact, redirect to a safe zone. Minimal movement, maximum survival.

🔓 Unlock Criteria

○
Complete Level 1 (Ball Striking)
○
Attend 2 Baby Block clinics
○
Demo: 3 consecutive blocks from a fed attack (ball stays in kitchen)
○
Demonstrate "let it go" decision on wide balls

Concept The Attack Lane

Where attacks go and why. Understanding the lane narrows your job.

Drill Feed & Block

Partner feeds attacks; you block to the safe zone. No backswing.

Drill Speed Read

Decide: block it or let it go? Train the read, not just the hands.

Assessment Banger vs Blocker

Live rally: partner bangs, you block. Survive 10 exchanges.

Level 1 — Ball Striking

Pickleball rewards short, simple strokes. Someone without a racket background can be genuinely good. Simpler motion, fewer errors, faster hands. This levels the playing field.

🔓 Unlock Criteria

✓
Complete Level 0 (Foundation)
○
Attend 2 Ball Striking clinics
○
Demo: 10 consecutive clean contacts with proper grip
○
Pass the 90-Second A/B Test (choke-up grip vs standard)

Concept The Big Insight

Why simpler strokes win. The physics of a short motion.

Drill Grip & Contact

Choke-up finger grip. Setup position. Clean contact zone.

Drill 90-Second A/B Test

Try both grips. See the difference yourself. Then decide.

Assessment Clean 10

10 consecutive clean contacts — no frame, no off-center. Simple pass/fail.
