How it works

You lost to the banger.
Here's why.

You just walked off the court. You lost to a banger. You know exactly why — you kept swinging at balls that were going out, and you handed them the match.

Or a pro just told you the same thing in a lesson. Or, you're about to step on the court against a banger-team and you need to prime your brain.

Either way, you already know the problem. Knowing it isn't the hard part. Rewiring your brain is.

This module trains the swing-or-let-it-go decision — the one read that keeps bangers from feeding off your over-swings. You get into ready position. The ball comes. You call it early. Over reps, your brain gets faster at that call.

Additional Resources
A. SHOULDER HEIGHT IQ — If the ball contacts above shoulder at pace, it's almost always out. Contact point is your leading cue.
B. THE WIND-UP CUE — A big paddle wind-up = pace. Big pace + high contact = out. Read the wind-up before the ball crosses the net.
C. READ THE SOURCE — Watch where the ball is being struck: deep in the court + hard swing = likely out. Kitchen shot + soft touch = stay ready to volley or let it bounce.
